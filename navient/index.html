As Navient Lawsuits Continue, Navient Student Loan Complaints Decrease

An overview of Navient's lawsuits, responses, and number of complaints from the best ranked states based on overall education.

Student loans are a process of borrowing money for post-secondary education either through the government or other organizations and institutions. These loans are vital to families who can not afford another year of education and offers relief to repay these loans overtime. Since 2017, Navient Corporation represents one of the nation’s largest federal student loan services but has been facing numerous lawsuits regarding their deceptive student loan practices.


The initial lawsuit began in January 2017 when the Consumer Financial Protection Bureau filed a lawsuit accusing Navient of not acting accordingly with customers. Shortly after, several other lawsuits were filed involving state attorney generals and teachers. In June 2020, a final settlement was reached stating that borrowers who were affected by the “widespread unfairness” are eligible for direct relief.


When taking out student loans, it is crucial to not only consider the school’s beneficial factors, but the overall ranking of the state’s education system. Education among the state can be considered a deciding factor for some students who are pursuing a post-secondary education. In the beginning of 2017, Naivent complaints saw a spike across the nation in student loan complaints, most likely due to the lawsuit. Fornuately the following month, Navient received far less complaints and has not seen a spike like that since. The only state to increase in complaints was Washington and the only state that does not qualify for a restitution payment is Utah. Due to the overall decrease, I do not believe that Navient's lawsuit should be ongoing due to their responsiveness to resolve the issue and how Navient is servicing the correct loans to each family.
